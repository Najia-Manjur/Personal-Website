MSc Thesis

(ongoing)

The impact of users' personality, mood and the design of the rating scales on their rating behavior

Supervisor: Dr. Julita Vassileva, Professor, Department of Computer Science, University of Saskatchewan

Rating scales play a vital role in collecting the post consumption feedback data from the users of a recommender system to offer each user a personalized experience. Sometimes the behavioral pattern of the user and the design or the emotional connotation of the rating scales can potentially manipulate user's mindset, resulting in a biased feedback. Understanding the influential factors behind such behavior is important in order to keep the efficiency of the system intact.

We implemented a web-based experiment platform to assess the personality and mood of the users (by using BFI-44 items and PANAS scale) and collect movie ratings from them. By analyzing the data collected from the user study, we aim to observe how the design of the rating scale can influence the rating behavior of the user and the relation between the personality and mood of the user and such biased behavior.

Work in progress
Analyzing Factors Influencing User Contribution and Predicting Involvement of Users on Stack Overflow

Stack Overflow may appear intimidating to new users which may explain why more than half of its users contribute to the community only once. In order to identify the dominant factors influencing such behavior, we analyzed the responses received by the new users. Our analysis showed that, getting no or minimal answers and comments, receiving downvotes and getting demeaning comments from their counterparts can act as the underlying reasons behind the decline in the participation of new users, however upvotes do not play any such significant role. We leveraged these factors and developed a model which performed significantly well in predicting the future involvement of the users.

Smart-NDA: A cloud based framework for smart device integrated automated neurodevelopmental disorder screening tool
International Conference on Medical Engineering, Health Informatics and Technology (MediTec) 2016

In Bangladesh, the detection of neurodevelopmental disorder gets delayed due to the lack of awareness and resources. Hence, proper initiative is not taken timely which causes permanant distress to the child. We proposed a cloud based, smart device integrated framework to screen such disability, which takes advantage of the questionnaires of RNDA with illustrations. Our tool is expected to increase the rate of early detection of neurodevelopmental disorder in Bangladesh.
